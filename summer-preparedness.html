  • Heat exhaustion recognition ‚Äî Heavy sweating, weakness, nausea

  • Light colors ‚Äî Reflect heat and sunlight

  • Lightweight materials ‚Äî Cotton or linen

Water Needs Calculation

  • Normal conditions ‚Äî 2-3 liters per person per day
  • Heat wave conditions ‚Äî 4-6 liters per person per day
  • Heavy work ‚Äî Up to 8 liters per person per day
  • Group planning ‚Äî Add 20% buffer for emergencies

Water Collection Techniques

  • Dew collection ‚Äî Cloth wicks and collection tarps
  • Solar stills ‚Äî Evaporation and condensation systems
  • Rainwater harvesting ‚Äî Storm collection systems
  • Tree tap methods ‚Äî Birch, maple water collection

Water Storage and Preservation

  • Underground storage ‚Äî Cooler temperatures reduce evaporation
  • Dark containers ‚Äî Prevent algae growth
  • Rotation system ‚Äî Use oldest water first
  • Purification maintenance ‚Äî Heat increases contamination risk

Water Rationing Protocols

  • Priority system: drinking > cooling > food prep > cleaning
  • Individual daily allocations based on role and health
  • Emergency reserves (20% of total never touched)
  • Group monitoring and accountability systems
